About me

As a PhD candidate at Université Hassan First – ISSS, I specialize in the ethnobotanical, pharmacological, and toxicological study of Moroccan medicinal plants, supported by the CNRST PhD-Associate Scholarship (PASS). My research bridges traditional plant knowledge and modern pharmacology, combining field ethnobotanical surveys with advanced analytical techniques.

I completed an international mobility research internship at Artois University (France) from October 2025 to January 2026, where I further developed my skills in extraction, phytochemical characterization, and bioactivity evaluation of plant extracts. I am currently seeking laboratory collaborations to conduct in vitro cytotoxicity assays on cancer cell lines, a crucial step to strengthen the scientific impact of my doctoral research.


What I do

  • Scientific Research

    Conducting doctoral research on medicinal plants used in oncology in Morocco, integrating ethnobotanical surveys, phytochemical profiling, and pharmacological evaluation to identify promising anticancer candidates from Moroccan flora.

  • Ethnobotany & Field Surveys

    Designing and conducting surveys with traditional herbalists across Morocco to document plant use in cancer-related treatments, bridging indigenous knowledge with evidence-based science.

  • Phytochemical Analysis

    Performing extraction, fractionation, and characterization of plant bioactive compounds using UHPLC-MS/MS, alongside biochemical and antioxidant assays.

  • Academic Teaching & Mentoring

    Supporting university education as a Doctoral Teaching Assistant at ISSS, supervising laboratory and tutorial sessions, assisting in examinations, and contributing to scientific event organization.
